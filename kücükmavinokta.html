Perseverance Rover

Neil Armstrong, the first person to walk on the moon, saw exactly this effect when he saw the world from there. “I suddenly realized that that little blue pea grain was our world,” Armstrong says. Then he closed one eye and lifted his thumb and put it on the world. At that moment, he felt very, very small that he would feel like a giant while doing this.

Sultan bin Salman Al Saud, who took to the space, describes his journey as follows: “On the first day, we all pointed to our own countries. On the 3rd or 4th day we were showing each other the continents. By the 5th day, we saw only one world." An astronaut who investigated whether there is a response or an explanation for his experiences and feelings in space, came across the concept of "Savikalpa Samadhi". This is a spiritual level of consciousness achieved by the destruction of the human ego. To destroy yourself temporarily and start seeing through the eyes of other beings.In order to have a cosmic consciousness, sometimes it is necessary to try to see through their eyes. Being able to take a step back from life and look at the whole picture. Just like the astronomer Carl Sagan did.When he saw this photo taken by the Voyager 1 spacecraft from a distance of about 6 billion km from the earth in 1990, the famous astronomer said:

“Look back at that point. That's our home. It is us. Everyone you love and know, whose name you have heard, living and dead are on him. The sum of all our joy and sorrow, thousands of religious, ideology and economics doctrines that contradict each other; every hunter and gatherer living throughout human history, every hero and coward, every founder and destroyer of civilization, every king and farmer, every lover couple, every mother and father, hopeful child, inventor, explorer, moral teacher, corrupt politician, every superstar , every “supreme leader”, every saint and sinner above him - that speck of dust suspended above a beam of sunlight. ” Astronomy is said to give people a humble personality. Perhaps there is no better picture of how stupid human arrogance is. We have to learn to look at each other through the eyes of others and behave better. To each other and to this pale blue point ...
