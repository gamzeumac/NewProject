PERSEVERANCE PROJECT

What would it be like to be all alone in the world…? Many TV series / movies have been shot about this. One of the funniest “The Last Man on Earth” Something is happening as usual, we are tampering with the settings of the world, and somehow all of the people on earth are disappearing in this series. One except person. It is this story that tells about that person's struggle for survival in a post-apocalyptic world. This time, not terribly, but in a funny way. What good is an advanced civilization if it doesn't have people left in it? Alone in the empty streets playing bowling What can you do other than punch holes in cars? The effect of this drama, which I can say is not very, but a little funny, was that it made me think seriously the following question: Who is the loneliest person in the world right now? Humanity does not have to be destroyed for this problem to be valid. I'm sure there is such a person; I searched the subject with the thought of someone who had asked this question before and indeed I found the loneliest person in the world currently alive. The man is sleeping under huge tropical leaves in one of the most remote corners of the world while you watch this video, which reaches your seat via one of the world's most advanced distribution networks, from a tiny screen you carry in your pocket, or a huge screen that you hang on a wall. Insects have bitten almost all over his half-naked body; giant flies fly around to taste his blood. Spider monkeys are running javelin on the top of the tree on which he rests. When he wakes up soon, he will try to hunt them to eat for breakfast. In order to cook it, he has to cut trees. If you listen carefully enough, you can hear the primitive ax swinging at the tree. I know it sounds like a fairy tale to you. Describing that humanity lived thousands of years ago as hunter-gatherer from such books as if I was reading a passage… By the way, Sapiens' comic was also published. It's like I'm showing an imaginary drawing inside this. But no. All of what I'm telling is true. You are currently watching imagery the only proof of the existence of this loneliest man in the world.

It is estimated that he is in his 50s. Turkey 7 lives alone in the depths of the Amazon jungle as big. He has not even stepped into the modern world until today. It has never come out of an area of approximately 50 square kilometers, which it has designated as a living space. How do we know all this? Established to protect Amazon Indians Funai This non-profit organization, which defends the rights of the indigenous people and has identified 113 different tribes living in isolation from the modern world so far. Most of these tribes disappear when they come into contact with the modern world. They die because they are not used to our illnesses and sick behaviors. That's why Since the 90's, some legal regulations have been introduced that protect them. Although the number of such tribes is very low, they still continue to struggle to exist in groups of 20-30 people. One way or another, 20-30 people are not considered alone because they are together. But our world's loneliest man is not like that. Unlike the others, he is alone. We do not know the language and name he speaks. We will probably never learn. All we know is that he digs holes about 2 meters deep in the ground. That's why they gave him a name for this feature. Hole man We don't know why he was digging these holes. There are various theories. Some claim that he set up a trap to hunt animals, some also he claims it has a religious purpose. Far from it, Africa people on the continent are also starting to dig holes in the ground to revitalize the ecosystem. heard but I don't really think that's why he's digging a hole where the hole man lives. We are not likely to ask him, because thanks to the measures taken by Funai, it is forbidden to enter his area. No other person can travel within 50 km of the loneliest man in the world anymore.

Shall I tell you something more interesting than that? About 50 years ago there was another man much more lonely and isolated than the hole man. The loneliest person in the universe, not the world. As you can imagine, I am talking about an astronaut who was sent to the Moon in 1969. No, Neil Armstrong wasn't the first to set foot on the moon, the loneliest person. Because there was Buzz Aldrin next to him in the vehicle that landed on the moon. So two people landed together. They set out as three people on the module that took them to the Moon, but when they reached the orbit of the Moon, two people landed with the "Eagle" module, while one person stayed in the "Columbia" module around the Moon to wait for them. Alone. He waited for 27 hours for his friends on the Moon to come back. Of course, he was busy communicating with both the control center on Earth and the Moon during this time. But since it rotates in its orbit, when it goes to the back of the Moon, it stops communicating for about 50 minutes. Here's the astronaut during that 50-minute period Michael Collins He earned the title of being a more isolated, distant, more lonely person than all people who have ever lived on earth. Think about it, the two closest to you are on the other side of the Moon, the rest of humanity is on Earth. I also want to add the title of "the most unlucky" to this. Because having to go up there, wander around the Moon for 27 hours and land on it while his friends pass through history, having to wait alone upstairs really makes one say "this is the luck". How big a distance is there between the hole man and this astronaut, right? Both of these people are alive now. In order for someone to be the loneliest person in the world, they should not go even 50 km from their birthplace. In order for the other to be the loneliest human being in the universe, humanity had to wait for the most advanced technological tools to be developed over thousands of years and travel 400,000 km with them. Two ends, the result of which is loneliness. But one of these two men, both still alive, was left alone knowingly and willingly. Although it doesn't land on the moon his prize was to go into the history of space. What about the other? Do you know why the hole man alone? Not because he chose to be that way. Once upon a time he also had a family. Although it consists of 20-30 people, it is a tribe. They lived in their own way, unaware of the outside world and civilization. They may not have discovered civilization, but civilization was quick to find the places where they lived. For a handful of land, they dived into the forest where they lived and destroyed that tribe along with the trees. Our hole man lost his entire family and tribe, everyone he knew in this world when he was just a kid in the 80's. I think it is more correct to call him a hole man for this reason, not because of the holes he dug into the ground. Because improving does not always mean progress. Sometimes many holes are opened in the heart of nature for this cause.
