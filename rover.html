Perseverance Rover

On February 19, 2021, we watched the "Perseverance rover", the largest, most advanced rover ever sent to another planet, as millions of people hold our breath and land on Mars. In fact, we listened to the signals he sent from a distance of 472 million km and received the information that it had arrived safely. I will not call him "perseverance" now, our sincerity increased, I will be content with simply saying "Percy". As soon as we landed on Mars, he started sending us very low resolution pictures. The pictures he recorded with the 23 cameras he sent in these 5 Mars days number approached 5000. So far, almost everything is as we expected. Communication was made in the same way as the vehicles sent before, and they also took pictures and sent them. But this time, what's sent is so clear Even the engineers working in the projed cannot believe their eyes. Now, we will first watch these clearest images from another planet to date, listen to the sound recorded on a planet for the first time, and most importantly, seek answers to the questions we are curious about. Watching Displays the picture published by NASA. A total of 23 cameras placed on different parts of the vehicle were recorded during the landing, descending and landing processes. First we see the parachute that opens after entering the Martian atmosphere. Real-time images on the right are shown as slowed down by 30% on the left because they are realized very quickly. We see that the speed of the vehicle is greatly reduced after the parachute is opened. The bottom cover of the heat shield that protects Percy from the effects of entering space and atmosphere is discarded. Now the speed of the vehicle is "subsonic", that is, slower than the speed of sound. When the lower cover is opened, radar and sub cameras begin to see and examine the surface of Mars for the first time. The ground clearance is currently 9.5 km. Now Percy and the celestial crane to which he was attached began to approach the ground at 83 meters per second. From here on We see that the vehicle starts to slide itself from the rough terrain on the right to the left. The place to land was pinpointed and the speed dropped to 30 meters per second. With 20 meters to the ground, the sky crane maneuver begins. This is the first time we are watching a landing on another planet from three different angles. The lower left image is recorded by the winch in the upper left and sent to Percy by the cable wrapped in 6 meters of ropes. Percy is also recording the crane with the camera on the top and the Martian soil with the camera below. Currently, the crane is trying to gently lower the rover to the ground at a height of about 20 meters from the ground. After the lowering has taken place, it leaves, releasing the cables and ropes. How were high resolution videos sent?

There is a small computer and storage unit on the Rover, called the Data Storage Unit (DSU). Nearly 30 GB of raw image was recorded by 6 cameras inside the Rover. With the help of the same computer, the most important parts were selected and compressed into small video clips. Thus, they significantly reduced the amount of data sent to Earth. There are three antennas above Percy. One of them is a 400 megahertz UHF antenna. It can provide fast data communication with satellites orbiting Mars. Distance between Rover and these satellites a conversation-like system with a walkie-talkie as it is so close they established. Later, these satellites send data to the world with antennas suitable for long-range communication on them. Deep Space Network (DSN) antennas spread across two hemispheres and three different regions of the world send these signals for 5 to 20 minutes depending on the size of the distance between Earth and Mars. can catch.
